Downtown Soccer Fest returns for a three-week summer run

Posted June 13, 2026 · Office of the Mayor

White Plains is transforming downtown into a celebration of the global game. On three Saturdays this summer, residents and visitors can watch international matchups on large screens in the heart of downtown — free and open to all.

⚽

Building on last year's turnout, the City's Soccer Fest brings live screenings, food vendors, and family activities to the downtown core. Watch parties are scheduled for June 14, June 27, and July 11, each running from late afternoon into the evening as matches conclude.

The events are organized by the City in partnership with the downtown business district, and are designed to draw foot traffic to local restaurants and shops during the summer months.

What to expect

Each watch party features a large LED screen broadcasting the day's featured match, with seating areas, food and beverage vendors, and activities for younger fans. Attendees are encouraged to arrive early, as space fills quickly once matches begin.

Street closures: Portions of Main Street and Mamaroneck Avenue will be closed to traffic during event hours. Free parking for vehicles registered to White Plains residents will be available in the Hamilton-Main Garage on event days.

Getting there

The festival site is a short walk from the White Plains Metro-North station and is served by several Bee-Line bus routes, which now accept tap-to-pay through OMNY. Residents driving in should plan for the announced street closures and use the designated resident parking.

For the full schedule, featured matches, and vendor information, residents can subscribe to City alerts or check this page as event dates approach.

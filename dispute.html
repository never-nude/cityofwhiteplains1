Dispute a charge or bill

Think a City charge is wrong? Tell us what and why. Most disputes are resolved without a formal proceeding — here's how it works and how to start.

What you can dispute here

Water & sewer bill

A reading that looks too high, a leak adjustment, or a billing error.

A City fee or invoice

Permit, license, or other charge you believe is incorrect or duplicated.

A parking ticket

Contest a citation you believe was issued in error.

Property assessment is different. If you disagree with your home's assessed value (not a bill), that goes through the Assessor's grievance process. Start with the Assessor →

How a dispute works

Submit your dispute

Use the form below with the bill or ticket number and what you think is wrong. There's no fee to ask.

Staff review

The right department checks the account, the reading or citation, and any notes, and may contact you for more detail.

You get a decision

You'll receive an adjustment, an explanation, or — for tickets — the option to escalate to a hearing.

Pay any corrected balance

If an amount remains, you can pay it online or set up a plan; due dates are paused while a dispute is open where allowed.

Start a dispute

We'll send your case number and any decision here.

Submissions in this proposal aren't processed; in production, this routes to the right department.

Frequently asked

Do I have to pay while a dispute is open?

Where the law allows, the disputed amount's due date is held while staff review. Anything not in dispute should still be paid on time to avoid penalties.

What if I disagree with the decision?

Parking citations can be escalated to a hearing; billing decisions can be reviewed by the department head. Your decision notice explains the next step.

How long does it take?

Simple billing corrections are often resolved within a few business days; tickets and complex cases take longer.

History & heritage

On July 9, 1776, the New York Provincial Congress met at the County Court House here, adopted the Declaration of Independence, and declared New York an independent state — which is why White Plains is called the Birthplace of the State of New York. The Battle of White Plains followed that October, and Battle Hill still carries Revolutionary War markers today.

Downtown White Plains

Mamaroneck Avenue is the spine of the city's cultural life — restaurants and rooftops, galleries and shops, street festivals and farmers markets, all a short walk from the train. Here's what's happening and how to make a day of it.

The heart of it: Mamaroneck Avenue

Running through the center of the city, Mamaroneck Avenue is where White Plains comes out to eat, meet, and celebrate. Its sidewalks string together restaurants and bars, coffee shops, boutiques, and arts spaces, and on warm evenings the street itself becomes the venue. It's walkable, transit-friendly, and the natural first stop whether you're new in town or have been here for years.

Eat & drink

Downtown's dining runs from quick weekday lunches to date-night dinners and late drinks — Mamaroneck Avenue and the blocks around it hold everything from family-friendly mainstays to newer rooftop and craft spots. Because the lineup changes with the seasons, the Business Improvement District keeps the most current directory.

Shop

You can spend an afternoon between the avenue's independent shops and the major centers nearby. The Westchester and City Center anchor downtown retail and dining — City Center also has a movie theater — while Mamaroneck Avenue keeps the local, one-of-a-kind storefronts that give downtown its character.

Arts & culture

White Plains is the cultural hub of the county, and much of that energy lives downtown. ArtsWestchester, the region's arts council, runs a gallery and programs from its home at 31 Mamaroneck Avenue, and the city's calendar fills out with concerts in the parks, public art, and seasonal performances throughout the year.

Signature downtown events

A rolling calendar of free, family-friendly happenings keeps downtown busy from spring through the holidays. A few favorites:

  • May–Aug3rd

    Rock the Block

    Third Wednesdays, May through August — live music, outdoor dining, and family fun in the heart of downtown.

  • Jun–Jul⚽

    Downtown Soccer Fest

    A free, citywide watch party inspired by the 2026 World Cup, turning downtown into a fan zone. Read more →

  • Summer🍗

    Wing Walk

    A downtown crawl of wing tastings from local restaurants, hosted by the BID.

  • Jun–Oct🥕

    Farmers Market

    Wednesdays on Court Street — local produce, bakers, and makers through the season.

  • Dec🎄

    Holiday Tree Lighting

    Music, hot cocoa, and a visit from Santa at Renaissance Plaza to kick off the season.

Getting here & parking

Downtown is built around the train. Metro-North's Harlem Line reaches White Plains in about 35 minutes from Grand Central, and the White Plains TransCenter connects Bee-Line buses right beside the station. If you drive, the City's parking garages put you a block or two from the avenue.

  • By train: Metro-North to White Plains — roughly 35 minutes from Manhattan.
  • By bus: Westchester's Bee-Line and OMNY tap-to-pay at the TransCenter.
  • By car: City garages and on-street parking throughout downtown.

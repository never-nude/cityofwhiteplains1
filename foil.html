Records & FOIL requests

New York's Freedom of Information Law (FOIL) gives you the right to access most records the City keeps. Here's what you can request, what to expect, and how to file.

What FOIL is

The Freedom of Information Law (Public Officers Law, Article 6) presumes that government records are open to the public. You don't have to explain why you want a record, and you don't have to be a resident. The City's Records Access Officer, in the Office of the City Clerk, handles these requests.

What you can request

  • Common Council agendas, minutes, and resolutions.
  • Contracts, budgets, and financial records.
  • Permits, certificates of occupancy, and property records.
  • Correspondence, reports, and other existing City documents.

What may be withheld

A few categories are exempt under the law, including records that would be an unwarranted invasion of personal privacy, certain law-enforcement materials, and information specifically exempted by statute. If part of a record is exempt, the City redacts that part and releases the rest.

Fees & timelines

  • Acknowledgment: the City responds within 5 business days of receiving your request — granting it, denying it, or telling you when to expect the records.
  • Copies: up to 25¢ per page for paper copies; electronic records are usually provided at no charge.
  • Format: if a record exists electronically, you can ask to receive it electronically.

Request records

Use the form below to file a FOIL request. Be as specific as you can — the records you want, and a date range if you know it — so the Clerk can find them quickly.

This proposal doesn't file official requests; in production, the Records Access Officer receives this and acknowledges within 5 business days.

If your request is denied

If a request is denied in whole or in part, you have the right to appeal in writing to the City's FOIL Appeals Officer within 30 days. The City must decide the appeal within 10 business days. If the appeal is denied, you may seek review from the State Committee on Open Government or in court.

Water & sewer

Pay your bill, look up your account, see current rates, and get help with a leak or an unexpectedly high bill.

Pay your water & sewer bill

Water and sewer service is billed quarterly. You can pay online by e-check (free) or by credit or debit card, by mail, or in person at City Hall.

Pay online → Set up autopay →

Look up your account

Enter your account number to see your current balance, due date, and most recent meter reading. Your account number is printed at the top of your quarterly statement.

Current quarterly rates

Charges combine a fixed service charge with metered usage billed per 100 cubic feet (CCF). One CCF is about 748 gallons.

Reading your bill

Your meter measures water in cubic feet. Each quarter the City reads the meter, subtracts the prior reading, and bills the difference. Sewer charges are based on the same metered water, since most water that comes in eventually goes down the drain.

  • Service charge: a flat quarterly fee that keeps your account active and the system maintained.
  • Usage: what you actually used since the last reading, billed per CCF.
  • Sewer: applied to metered water use to cover treatment and the sewer system.

High bill or a possible leak?

A bill much higher than usual almost always means water is moving when you think it's off — most often a running toilet, which can waste thousands of gallons a quarter.

  • Check for silent leaks: note your meter reading at night, then again before using any water in the morning. If it moved, something is running.
  • Toilets first: add a few drops of food coloring to the tank; if color appears in the bowl without flushing, the flapper is leaking.
  • Request a re-read: if the reading looks wrong, Public Works can verify it.
  • Leak adjustments: after a documented repair, you may qualify for a one-time adjustment on the excess.

Starting or stopping service

Buying or selling a home, or moving in or out? Contact Public Works to transfer service and request a final reading. A final read at closing makes sure each owner pays only for the water they used.

How City hiring works

White Plains is a civil-service employer under New York State Civil Service Law. That system is built to make government hiring fair and based on merit. Most positions fall into one of two tracks:

  • Competitive class: filled from eligible lists created by civil-service examinations. To be hired, you generally take an exam, earn a place on the list, and are reachable when a vacancy is filled.
  • Non-competitive & labor class: many maintenance, labor, and certain skilled positions don't require an exam and are filled directly based on qualifications.

The hiring process, step by step

  1. Find a position

    Browse current openings and read the job specification for duties, minimum qualifications, and salary.

  2. Apply or file for an exam

    Submit an application for an open posting, or file for the civil-service exam tied to the title you want.

  3. Take the exam

    For competitive titles, sit for the written and/or practical exam. You'll receive a score and, if you pass, a place on the eligible list.

  4. Eligible list & interview

    When a vacancy opens, the department interviews candidates reachable from the list under the "rule of three."

  5. Appointment

    Selected candidates are appointed, typically with a probationary period, then become permanent.

Benefits of working for the City

  • New York State & Local Retirement System (pension) membership.
  • Comprehensive health, dental, and vision coverage.
  • Paid holidays, vacation, personal, and sick leave.
  • Deferred-compensation savings options and life insurance.
  • Stable, mission-driven public service close to home in Westchester.

Equal opportunity

The City of White Plains is an Equal Opportunity Employer. The City does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, sex, age, disability, or any other protected status, and provides reasonable accommodations in the application and examination process on request.

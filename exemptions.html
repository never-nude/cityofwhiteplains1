Property tax exemptions

Exemptions lower the taxable value of your home, which lowers your City property tax bill. See what you may qualify for, the deadline, and how to apply.

Exemptions you may qualify for

Basic & Enhanced STAR

School-tax relief for owner-occupied homes; Enhanced STAR adds savings for seniors who meet the income limit.

Senior Citizens

Owners 65+ with income below the City limit may receive a reduction in assessed value.

Veterans

Alternative and Cold War veterans exemptions for those who served, with added benefit for service-connected disabilities.

Persons with Disabilities

Owners with a documented disability and limited income may qualify for a reduction.

Clergy

A statutory exemption for members of the clergy who meet the requirements.

Home Improvement

Temporary relief on the added value of certain residential improvements.

How to apply

Confirm you're eligible

Each exemption has its own age, income, residency, or service requirements. Use the form below and the Assessor will tell you what fits.

Gather your documents

Typically proof of age, income (prior-year tax return), residency, and — for veterans — discharge papers (DD-214).

File by the deadline

Most exemption applications are due by the City's taxable status date in the spring. File once; some renew automatically, others annually.

See it on your bill

Approved exemptions appear on the next assessment roll and reduce the tax you owe.

Check what you may qualify for

Tell the Assessor a little about your situation and they'll follow up with the exemptions you're likely eligible for and the forms you need.

Which may apply to you?

This proposal doesn't process applications; in production, the Assessor receives this and replies with next steps.

Frequently asked

What's the deadline to apply?

Most exemptions must be filed by the taxable status date set each spring. Applying after that date generally means the exemption starts the following tax year.

Do I have to reapply every year?

Basic STAR and some others carry over automatically; income-based exemptions like Senior Citizens usually require annual renewal. The Assessor will tell you which applies.

Can I combine exemptions?

Often yes — for example, a senior may receive both Enhanced STAR and the Senior Citizens exemption if income limits are met.

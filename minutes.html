1. Call to order & roll call

Mayor Brasch called the regular meeting to order at 7:30 p.m. and led the Pledge of Allegiance.

Present: Mayor Justin C. Brasch (presiding), Common Council President John M. Martin, and the six members of the Common Council. Absent: None. A quorum was present.

2. Approval of previous minutes

The Council reviewed the minutes of the regular meeting of May 4, 2026. President Martin moved to approve the minutes as submitted; the motion was seconded.

Adopted 7–0

3. Public comment

The Mayor opened the public comment period. Three residents spoke. Comments addressed summer pool hours at the Gardella and Kittrell facilities, pedestrian safety near the Bryant–Mamaroneck StoryWalk, and support for expanded weekend Bee-Line service. The Mayor directed staff to follow up with each speaker.

4. Public hearing — proposed 2026–2027 City budget

The Council held a public hearing on the proposed municipal budget for fiscal year 2026–2027. The Commissioner of Finance summarized proposed appropriations and the projected tax levy. Two residents offered comment. The hearing was continued to the meeting of June 15, 2026 to allow additional written comment.

Action: Hearing continued (no vote required).

5. Resolution — Kittrell Pool deck resurfacing

A resolution to award a contract for resurfacing the deck at Kittrell Pool, in advance of the summer season, was presented by the Department of Recreation & Parks. The work is to be completed under the department's capital maintenance program.

Adopted 7–0

6. Resolution — Bee-Line / OMNY coordination grant

A resolution authorizing the City to accept and administer a county transit-coordination grant supporting OMNY tap-to-pay rollout on Bee-Line routes serving downtown White Plains was presented. The grant requires no local match.

Adopted 7–0

7. Ordinance — Hamilton-Main Garage parking schedule

An ordinance updating the posted rate and permit schedule for the Hamilton-Main Garage, including a resident evening rate and event-day provisions for downtown programming, was introduced. Following discussion, the Council adopted the ordinance with one member voting in opposition.

Adopted 6–1

8. Resolution — StoryWalk seasonal installation

A resolution funding the next seasonal title installation along the Bryant–Mamaroneck StoryWalk trail, in partnership with the Public Library and the Youth Bureau, was presented.

Adopted 7–0

9. Adjournment

There being no further business, the Mayor adjourned the meeting at 8:52 p.m. The next regular meeting is scheduled for Monday, June 15, 2026 at 7:30 p.m.

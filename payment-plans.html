Payment plan options

If you can't pay a City balance in full, a payment plan lets you pay it down in scheduled installments and avoid further collection action. Here's who qualifies, how it works, and how to request one.

Who can set up a plan

Payment plans are available to residents and property owners with an outstanding balance on:

  • Property taxes — current or delinquent City property tax bills
  • Water & sewer — past-due utility charges
  • Other City charges — most invoiced balances owed to the City

Accounts already in a tax-enforcement or legal proceeding may have different terms — the Finance Department will let you know when you ask.

How a payment plan works

Tell us about the balance

Request a plan below with your account or parcel number and the amount owed. There's no fee to ask.

Finance reviews & proposes terms

Staff confirm the balance and offer a schedule — typically 3, 6, or 12 monthly installments depending on the amount.

You approve & make the first payment

Once you accept the terms, your first installment starts the plan. Interest or statutory charges may still apply to unpaid balances.

Pay on schedule

Pay each installment online, by mail, or in person. Keeping to the schedule keeps the account in good standing.

Request a payment plan

Send the City the basics and Finance will follow up with proposed terms. You don't need an account to ask.

Frequently asked

Will a payment plan stop interest or penalties?

A plan helps you avoid further collection action, but statutory interest and penalties on unpaid tax balances are set by law and may continue until the balance is paid. Finance will spell out what applies to your account.

What if I miss an installment?

Contact Finance as soon as you can. A missed payment can put the account back into collections, but staff can often help you get back on track.

Can I pay the balance off early?

Yes. You can pay ahead or pay the remaining balance in full at any time with no penalty.

Is there a fee to set up a plan?

There's no fee to request or set up a standard payment plan. Card payments may carry the usual processing fee.
